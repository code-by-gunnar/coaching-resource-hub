Understanding different professional coaching standards is crucial for your development as a coach. This educational guide compares the two major coaching framework organizations to help you understand the industry landscape.

**Key Facts:**
- **Members:** 50,000+ coaches worldwide

- **Recognition:** Widely considered the "gold standard"
- **Focus:** Global standards and ethics

### **Association of Coaching (AC)** üá¨üáß
UK-based professional body founded in 2002, with strong European presence.

- **Members:** 8,000+ coaches globally

- **Recognition:** Strong in European markets
- **Focus:** Research-based practice and continuous development

## üîç Detailed Comparison: ICF vs AC

### **1. Certification Levels**

#### **ICF Credentials:**
- **ACC** (Associate Certified Coach) - Entry level
- **PCC** (Professional Certified Coach) - Intermediate
- **MCC** (Master Certified Coach) - Advanced

#### **AC Credentials:**
- **AC Practitioner** - Entry level
- **AC Member** - Standard professional level
- **AC Fellow** - Senior level recognition

### **2. Training Requirements**

| Aspect | ICF Requirements | AC Requirements |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Coach Education Hours** | 60+ (ACC), 125+ (PCC), 200+ (MCC) | 100+ (Practitioner), 200+ (Member) |
| **Mentor Coaching** | 10+ hours | 35+ hours |
| **Coaching Experience** | 100+ hours (ACC), 500+ (PCC), 2,500+ (MCC) | 100+ hours initial |
| **Continuing Education** | 40 hours every 3 years | 25 hours annually |

### **3. Assessment Process**

#### **ICF Assessment:**
- Written exam (ACC/PCC)
- Oral exam (MCC)
- Portfolio submission
- Recorded coaching sessions

#### **AC Assessment:**
- Portfolio-based assessment
- Reflective practice evidence
- Peer review process
- No standardized exam

### **4. Cost Comparison**

- **Application Fee:** $100-300

## üéØ Which Certification Should You Choose?

### **Choose ICF If You:**
- Want global recognition and credibility
- Plan to coach internationally
- Prefer structured, standardized requirements
- Value the prestige of the "gold standard"
- Need corporate recognition (many companies specify ICF)

### **Choose AC If You:**
- Are based in the UK or Europe
- Prefer a research-based, reflective approach
- Value peer review over standardized testing
- Want more flexible development pathways
- Prefer smaller, community-focused organization

### **Consider Both If You:**
- Want maximum credibility and options
- Coach in multiple markets
- Have the time and resources for dual certification
- Value different approaches to professional development

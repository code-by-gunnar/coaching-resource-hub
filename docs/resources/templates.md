## üöÄ Initial Coaching Sessions {#initial-coaching-sessions}

### **1. Discovery Session Template (60-90 minutes)**

**Purpose:** Build rapport, understand client needs, explain coaching process, and determine fit.

#### **Pre-Session Preparation (5 minutes)**
- Review client intake form and any pre-session materials
- Set up comfortable, distraction-free environment
- Prepare coaching agreement and relevant documents
- Clear your mind and set intention for the session

#### **Opening & Rapport Building (10-15 minutes)**
```
"Welcome! I'm excited to meet with you today. Before we dive in, 
I'd love to know - how are you feeling about this conversation? 
What's on your mind as we start?"

Key Elements:
‚ñ° Personal check-in and mood assessment
‚ñ° Address any nervousness or concerns
‚ñ° Explain session structure and timeline
‚ñ° Confirm confidentiality and create safety
```

#### **Intake & Background Exploration (20-25 minutes)**
```
Exploration Questions:
‚Ä¢ "Tell me about what brought you to coaching at this point in your life."
‚Ä¢ "What's working well in your life right now?"
‚Ä¢ "What areas would you like to see change or improvement?"
‚Ä¢ "What have you tried before to address these challenges?"
‚Ä¢ "When you imagine your life 6 months from now, what do you hope is different?"

Areas to Explore:
‚ñ° Current life situation and satisfaction levels
‚ñ° Professional background and career trajectory
‚ñ° Relationship dynamics and support systems
‚ñ° Health and wellness practices
‚ñ° Personal growth and development history
‚ñ° Previous coaching, therapy, or counseling experience
```

#### **Coaching Process Explanation (10-15 minutes)**
```
Topics to Cover:
‚ñ° What coaching is and isn't (vs. therapy, consulting, mentoring)
‚ñ° Your coaching philosophy and approach
‚ñ° Typical session structure and frequency
‚ñ° Client responsibilities and expectations
‚ñ° Coach responsibilities and boundaries
‚ñ° Confidentiality policies and exceptions
‚ñ° Payment terms and scheduling policies
```

#### **Goal Exploration & Prioritization (15-20 minutes)**
```
Initial Goal Discovery:
‚Ä¢ "If we could accomplish 3 things together over the next 6 months, 
  what would have the biggest positive impact on your life?"
‚Ä¢ "What would success look like for you in our coaching relationship?"
‚Ä¢ "Which of these areas feels most urgent or important to start with?"

Goal Prioritization Exercise:
‚ñ° List 5-7 potential coaching goals
‚ñ° Rate importance (1-10) and urgency (1-10)
‚ñ° Identify interdependencies between goals
‚ñ° Select 1-2 primary focus areas to begin
```

#### **Next Steps & Agreement (10-15 minutes)**
```
Session Closing:
‚ñ° Summarize key insights and priority areas
‚ñ° Explain coaching agreement terms
‚ñ° Schedule next session and establish frequency
‚ñ° Assign any pre-work or assessments
‚ñ° Exchange contact information and communication preferences
‚ñ° Address remaining questions or concerns

Client Homework:
‚Ä¢ Complete Values Assessment (if not done)
‚Ä¢ Reflect on session insights in journal
‚Ä¢ Identify one small action to take before next session
```

**Purpose:** Establish clear, compelling goals and initial action plans for coaching engagement.

#### **Session Structure:**

**Check-In & Reflection (10 minutes)**
```

‚Ä¢ "How have you been since our last session?"
‚Ä¢ "What insights or thoughts have come up for you?"
```

**Vision Exploration (20 minutes)**
```

‚Ä¢ "What time do you wake up and how do you feel?"

‚Ä¢ "How are your relationships?"
‚Ä¢ "What are you most proud of accomplishing?"
‚Ä¢ "What's different about how you feel about yourself?"
```

```
‚ñ° Break each goal into 90-day milestones
‚ñ° Identify weekly action steps
‚ñ° Anticipate obstacles and create contingency plans
‚ñ° Establish accountability measures
‚ñ° Schedule check-in points
```

**Commitment & Next Steps (10 minutes)**
```
Session Closing:
‚ñ° Client reads goals aloud to solidify commitment

‚ñ° Identify support person to share goals with
```

### **3. Standard 60-Minute Coaching Session**

**The most common coaching session structure for regular ongoing sessions.**

#### **Session Flow:**

**Opening Check-In (5-8 minutes)**
```

‚Ä¢ "How are you today?"
‚Ä¢ "What's been going on since we last spoke?"
‚Ä¢ "What would make this session valuable for you?"
‚Ä¢ "Is there anything urgent we need to address first?"

‚Ä¢ "On a scale of 1-10, what's your energy level?"
‚Ä¢ "What do you need to be fully present for our session?"
```

**Progress Review (8-12 minutes)**
```
Accountability Check:
‚Ä¢ "Let's review what you committed to doing since our last session."
‚Ä¢ "What did you accomplish?"
‚Ä¢ "What didn't get done and what got in the way?"
‚Ä¢ "What did you learn about yourself in the process?"

Celebration and Learning:
‚ñ° Acknowledge all progress, even small steps
‚ñ° Identify lessons learned from challenges
‚ñ° Notice patterns and growth areas
‚ñ° Adjust strategies based on what worked/didn't work
```

**Session Focus - GROW Model (35-40 minutes)**

**Goal (5-8 minutes)**
```
‚Ä¢ "What do you want to focus on today?"
‚Ä¢ "What outcome would make this session worthwhile?"
‚Ä¢ "How does this connect to your larger goals?"
```

**Reality (10-12 minutes)**
```
‚Ä¢ "What's the current situation with this?"
‚Ä¢ "What have you tried so far?"
‚Ä¢ "What's working? What isn't?"
‚Ä¢ "Who else is involved?"
‚Ä¢ "What obstacles are you facing?"
```

**Options (12-15 minutes)**
```
‚Ä¢ "What options do you see?"
‚Ä¢ "What else could you try?"
‚Ä¢ "If you had unlimited resources, what would you do?"
‚Ä¢ "What would you advise a friend in this situation?"
‚Ä¢ "What haven't we considered yet?"
```

**Will/Way Forward (8-10 minutes)**
```
‚Ä¢ "What will you do?"
‚Ä¢ "When will you do it?"
‚Ä¢ "What might get in the way and how will you handle that?"
‚Ä¢ "Who can support you?"
‚Ä¢ "How will you track progress?"
```

**Session Closing (5-7 minutes)**
```
Wrap-Up Process:
‚ñ° Summarize key insights and decisions
‚ñ° Confirm action commitments
‚ñ° Schedule next session
‚ñ° Ask for feedback on session quality
‚ñ° End with encouragement and affirmation

Closing Questions:
‚Ä¢ "What are your biggest takeaways from today?"
‚Ä¢ "What are you most excited about?"
‚Ä¢ "How are you feeling about your next steps?"
```

## üìä Specialized Session Types {#specialized-session-types}

### **5. Challenge/Problem-Solving Session**

**For addressing specific obstacles, conflicts, or complex decisions.**

#### **Solution-Focused Structure (60 minutes):**

**Problem Definition (10-12 minutes)**
```
Understanding the Challenge:
‚Ä¢ "Describe the situation as objectively as possible."
‚Ä¢ "What makes this particularly challenging for you?"
‚Ä¢ "How is this affecting other areas of your life?"
‚Ä¢ "What would need to change for this to no longer be a problem?"

Scaling the Problem:
‚Ä¢ "On a scale of 1-10, where 10 is 'completely overwhelming' 
  and 1 is 'no problem at all,' where is this challenge today?"
```

**Exception Exploration (12-15 minutes)**
```
Finding What Works:
‚Ä¢ "Tell me about a time when this problem was less severe or not present."
‚Ä¢ "What was different during that time?"
‚Ä¢ "What were you doing differently?"
‚Ä¢ "Who was involved and how?"
‚Ä¢ "How did you make that happen?"

Resources and Strengths:
‚Ä¢ "What strengths have helped you handle difficult situations before?"
‚Ä¢ "Who in your support network could help with this?"
‚Ä¢ "What resources do you have access to?"
```

**Solution Generation (20-25 minutes)**
```
Creative Problem-Solving:
‚Ä¢ "If you woke up tomorrow and this problem was completely solved, 
  what would be different?"
‚Ä¢ "What's the smallest step that would move you toward solution?"
‚Ä¢ "What would someone you admire do in this situation?"
‚Ä¢ "What haven't you tried yet that might work?"

Option Evaluation:
‚ñ° Generate 5-7 potential solutions
‚ñ° Evaluate feasibility and likely effectiveness
‚ñ° Consider resource requirements
‚ñ° Assess alignment with values and goals
‚ñ° Choose 1-2 to implement
```

**Action Planning & Implementation (8-10 minutes)**
```
Solution Implementation:
‚Ä¢ "Which solution will you try first?"
‚Ä¢ "What exactly will you do and when?"
‚Ä¢ "How will you know if it's working?"
‚Ä¢ "What support do you need?"
‚Ä¢ "What will you do if this doesn't work as planned?"
```

## üí° Template Customization Guidelines {#closure--transition-templates}

### **Adapting Templates to Your Style**

#### **Personalization Strategies:**
- **Language**: Adjust to match your natural communication style
- **Timing**: Modify time allocations based on your pace and client needs
- **Questions**: Add questions that reflect your coaching philosophy
- **Structure**: Reorganize elements to match your preferred flow
- **Tools**: Integrate with your favorite coaching tools and assessments

#### **Client Adaptation:**
- **Communication Style**: Adjust language for formal vs. casual clients
- **Processing Speed**: Allow more/less time based on client pace
- **Learning Style**: Emphasize visual, auditory, or kinesthetic elements
- **Cultural Considerations**: Respect cultural communication preferences
- **Industry Context**: Use relevant examples and language for their field

### **Quality Assurance Checklist**

#### **Before Each Session:**
‚ñ° Review template and customize for specific client
‚ñ° Prepare any materials or resources needed
‚ñ° Check technology and environment setup
‚ñ° Set personal intention and energy for session
‚ñ° Review client's previous session notes and commitments

#### **During Sessions:**
‚ñ° Stay flexible with template - follow client's needs
‚ñ° Track time to ensure all important elements are covered
‚ñ° Take notes for follow-up and future sessions
‚ñ° Notice what's working and what might need adjustment

#### **After Sessions:**
